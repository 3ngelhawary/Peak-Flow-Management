Calculation Concept

Rational mode: peak flow is calculated from Q = C × i × A / 360, then distributed using a simplified triangular hydrograph (Tp = storm duration / 2). Select intensity i from your local IDF curve at the duration equal to the system's Time of Concentration and your target return period (e.g. 10-year, 100-year).

SCS mode: 24-hour Type II cumulative rainfall is scaled to the input storm depth, converted to CN runoff, then routed through an NRCS dimensionless unit hydrograph. The user inputs Tc (Time of Concentration); the model internally derives Tp (Time to Peak) as Tp = (Δt/2) + 0.6 × Tc. Tc max is 360 min for a 24-hour storm.

Max Factored Volume: maximum storage reached during simulation × safety factor. This is the required tank design size.

Emptying Time: time to drain the actual peak volume (unfactored) at pump rate. Use this to verify inter-storm recovery.

Direct Inflow: a constant flow (L/sec) added to the storm hydrograph throughout the entire simulation. Use this to represent a forcemain, pump station discharge, or any external pipe disposing into the network at a fixed rate regardless of rainfall.

Overflow Volume: total volume lost when the tank exceeded capacity. A non-zero value means the tank is undersized or the pump is too slow.

Best storage duration: the critical duration is the one producing the highest max factored volume while emptying time remains acceptable.
