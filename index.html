Retention Tank

Concept

The displayed required storage is the maximum factored volume reached during the simulation, not the final remaining volume. Emptying time is calculated from that maximum volume divided by pump discharge.

To determine the best storage duration, run/check several storm durations and select the critical duration: the one that gives the highest Max Factored Volume while keeping overflow risk and emptying time acceptable.

Qpeak = C × i × A / 360

Vrequired = max[Σ(Qin − Qpump) × Δt × SF]

Calculation method: Rational Method is used to calculate peak inflow. A triangular hydrograph shape is then used to distribute that peak flow over the storm duration for dynamic storage simulation.

Tank Capacity is the physical available tank size entered by the user. Max Factored Volume is the calculated required storage during the storm, including safety factor. If Max Factored Volume is higher than Tank Capacity, the tank is undersized.

Pump Discharge is entered in L/sec for site use. The simulator converts it internally to m³/s using: m³/s = L/sec ÷ 1000.
